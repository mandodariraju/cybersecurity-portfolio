# Incident Handler Journal

## Journal Entry 1

**Date:** 16 June 2026
**Entry:** 1

### Description

This journal entry documents a ransomware incident that affected a healthcare clinic. The attack disrupted business operations and prevented employees from accessing critical patient records and organizational systems.

### Tool(s) Used

No cybersecurity tools were used during this initial incident review. Information was documented based on the incident scenario and analysis.

## The 5 W's

### Who

An organized cybercriminal group conducted the attack. The attackers used phishing emails containing malicious attachments to gain access to the organization's systems.

### What

A ransomware attack encrypted critical files and displayed a ransom note demanding payment for a decryption key. Employees were unable to access patient records and essential business applications.

### When

The incident occurred on a Tuesday morning at approximately 9:00 a.m. Employees reported issues accessing files and systems shortly after beginning work.

### Where

The attack affected the healthcare clinic's internal network, computer systems, and data resources. Critical patient information and operational systems were impacted.

### Why

The attackers successfully used phishing emails to trick employees into downloading malicious attachments. The malware installed ransomware that encrypted organizational files and disrupted business operations.

## Additional Notes

This incident demonstrates the importance of employee security awareness training, phishing detection, email filtering, endpoint protection, and regular data backups. Future investigations should determine the scope of affected systems, identify the initial compromised account, and verify whether sensitive patient data was accessed before encryption.

## Key Lessons Learned

* Phishing emails remain a common entry point for ransomware attacks.
* Employee cybersecurity awareness training is critical.
* Regular backups help organizations recover from ransomware incidents.
* Incident documentation supports effective response and recovery efforts.
* Early detection can reduce the overall impact of security incidents.
