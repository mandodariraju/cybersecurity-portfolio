# Ransomware Incident Journal

**Date:** 16 June 2026

**Entry:** 1

## Description

This journal entry documents a ransomware incident that affected a healthcare clinic. The attack disrupted business operations and prevented employees from accessing critical patient records after threat actors deployed ransomware through a phishing email campaign.

## Tool(s) Used

* Incident Handler's Journal
* Incident Documentation Process
* 5 W's Analysis Method

## The 5 W's

### Who

An organized group of cybercriminals caused the incident. The attackers targeted the healthcare clinic using phishing emails containing malicious attachments.

### What

A ransomware attack encrypted critical organizational files and displayed a ransom note demanding payment in exchange for a decryption key. Employees lost access to medical records and essential systems required for daily operations.

### When

The incident occurred on a Tuesday morning at approximately 9:00 a.m. Employees reported being unable to access files and business applications shortly after arriving at work.

### Where

The attack affected the healthcare clinic's internal computer systems and network infrastructure. Critical patient data and operational systems were impacted.

### Why

The attackers gained access through phishing emails that tricked employees into downloading malicious attachments. Once executed, the malware installed ransomware that encrypted important organizational files and disrupted business operations.

## Additional Notes

This incident highlights the importance of security awareness training, phishing detection, email security controls, and regular data backups. Further investigation would be needed to determine the number of affected systems, identify the initial point of compromise, and assess whether sensitive patient information was accessed before encryption occurred.
