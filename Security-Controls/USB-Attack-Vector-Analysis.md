# USB Attack Vector Analysis

## Contents

The USB drive contains a mix of personal and work-related information, including family photos, pet photos, a resume, a wedding planning document, employee shift schedules, and a new hire letter. Some of these files may contain personally identifiable information (PII) and sensitive organizational data. Storing personal and business information on the same device increases the risk of accidental data exposure and unauthorized access.

## Attacker Mindset

An attacker who obtains this USB drive could use the personal information to conduct social engineering or phishing attacks against Jorge Bailey, his family members, or hospital employees. Documents such as employee schedules, resumes, and hiring information could provide valuable intelligence for impersonation or targeted attacks. The attacker could also use the information to gain unauthorized access to organizational systems or exploit employee trust.

## Risk Analysis

Unknown USB devices may contain malicious software such as ransomware, spyware, keyloggers, or backdoors. If an infected USB drive were connected to a company device, it could compromise systems, steal sensitive information, or disrupt business operations. To reduce these risks, organizations should implement security awareness training, restrict the use of unauthorized USB devices, deploy endpoint protection software, and establish policies for handling removable media. Using isolated virtual environments to analyze unknown devices can further help prevent malware infections and protect organizational assets.
